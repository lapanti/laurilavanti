Finland has spent decades building a technological edge — from Vaisala and icebreakers to Nokia, Linux and Supercell. That lead is now under pressure, and at the same time AI is transforming work, digital services and human connection faster than legislators can respond.

Parliament needs people who understand technology from the inside. I have an engineering degree from Aalto University and over a decade of experience building secure software and banking services. I know how these systems work — and what happens when they don't.

These are my topics. The driving theme is AI. The three pillars are economy, education and freedom. Their synthesis is digital independence — Finland making its own technology decisions.
