Recent news has reported that a group of residents is investigating the separation of Veikkola from Kirkkonummi. The investigation reflects in part how disappointed residents are with the decision of Kirkkonummi municipal council to withdraw from the Western Rail Link project and reject one of the most significant development investments for Kirkkonummi in decades.

The planning of Northern Kirkkonummi has long rested on the possibility of a station. The key decision to withdraw from the Western Rail Link project — which concerned the station — was made, however, without an assessment of the decision's impacts and contrary to the proposal of the municipal officials. It is now known that the Western Rail Link municipalities and the state will continue the project without the Veikkola station. The possibility of further negotiations proposed by those who opposed the Western Rail Link agreement did not, in reality, exist, and Kirkkonummi will by all appearances bear the disadvantages of the Western Rail Link without the benefits.

> Despite assumptions, there are no separate negotiations with the Western Rail Link in sight.

Kirkkonummi has set as its goal to be the most desirable, vibrant and vital municipality in the metropolitan area. High-quality housing and functional connections to the capital region have long been among Kirkkonummi's most important attractions. The Northern Kirkkonummi station would have met the strategic objectives by offering housing close to services and good public transport, as well as new opportunities for business.

In making the decision, the ability of decision-makers to make strategically important, forward-looking decisions for the whole municipality was tested. The Greens was the only major party in Kirkkonummi of which the majority supported the Western Rail Link agreement and with it the Northern Kirkkonummi station. By contrast, only one of the SDP's councillors did so, and none of the councillors from the Swedish People's Party, Pro Kirkkonummi or the Finns Party, for example. Those of us who supported the project saw it as part of the municipality's long-term development and the future of all of Kirkkonummi. Now we decision-makers in Kirkkonummi have the opportunity to demonstrate that we can ensure the whole of Kirkkonummi remains vital in the future as well, and to restore the trust of Northern Kirkkonummi residents in decision-making.
